About

Artist Statement

My work is rooted in the human condition - memory, displacement, and emotional truth. I was born in Mostar and my early life was marked by the experience of war and constant movement, which shaped my sensitivity toward people, relationships, and fragility of existence.

Painting became my way of processing memory and emotion, but also a way of preserving what is often lost in language: silence, intimacy, and inner states. I work primarily in figurative painting, using the human figure as a carrier of psychological and emotional depth rather than a literal portrait.

In recent years, my practice has focused on themes of motherhood, collective trauma, and cultural memory, as well as deeply personal explorations of sevdah, understood not only as music, but as a state of longing, tenderness, and inner strength. Through this, I try to translate invisible emotional experiences into visual form.

My paintings are not answers, but open spaces, inviting the viewer into reflection, recognition, and quiet dialogue with themselves.

Biography

Nebojša Dimovski was born in Mostar in 1988. He finished art high school in Sarajevo, after which he went to Italy and entered the Accademia di Belle Arti di Roma, at La Sapienza University. He finishes painting in the class of professors Andrea Volo and Constantin Blandino. During his studies, one of his works was selected for the final of the prestigious Premio Nazionale delle Arti in Naples. After completing his studies, he went to the Netherlands, where he spent time in research work and in the studio of the American sculptor Barbara Greenberg. After Holland, he goes to Madrid, where he has notable exhibitions, such as the final of the 83rd Autumn Salon, Casa de Vacas, the Spring Salon, Valdepenas, a solo exhibition at the Association of Artists and Sculptors of Spain and many group exhibitions in Spain, Hungary, Italy, the Netherlands, Czech Republic, Serbia and the United States. He won the First Prize at the Valencia Kuna international competition.

Sevdisanje slikom

An exhibition born from a deeply personal, emotional experience. Through his own inner crises, Nebojša Dimovski arrives at a fateful encounter with sevdalinka — not as a musical form, but as a space of solace, a cry of the soul, and quiet inner strength. In this encounter, the artist connects with all the subtle souls who, across generations, gave birth to sevdalinka as one of the most valuable and profound traditional symbols of the people in this region. The scenes that appear on the canvases are not illustrations, but emotional echoes — fragments of states, atmospheres, and memories evoked from verses and melodies. This is not an exhibition about music. This is an exhibition about the memories of the soul. This is an exhibition about sevdah and sevdisanje.
